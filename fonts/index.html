Maggie Cocco Music

Maggie Cocco is a Southern born, Detroit raised, San Francisco based singer-songwriter. Fans of singer-songwriters from the 60s to today will love her “sincere and timeless” style. Since 2015 Maggie has performed and released original music under the moniker “Science for Sociopaths” for audiences here. “There’s this fearless expression of sentiment and truth, coiled into rhymed lyrics that bridge indie-pop to a soulful blues-rock” [Jeff Milo, DeepCutz 2018]. When not touring, Maggie divides her time between teaching music to adults and children, and applying her talents to various live and studio collaborations in the San Francisco Bay area. Sounds Like: Sheryl Crowe, Melissa Etheridge, Carole King, Joni Mitchell

Science for Sociopaths

Science for Sociopaths is an introspective, soulful singer-songwriter project from southern born, Detroit raised, San Francisco based artist Maggie Cocco. Appealing to fans of singer-songwriters from the 60s to today, Maggie’s originals meld licks like Sheryl Crowe, power like Melissa Etheridge, and depth like Brandi Carlisle. Her timeless songs and sincere performances have been engaging fans and critics alike since her emergence in the Detroit scene in 2015. “There’s this fearless expression of sentiment and truth, coiled into rhymed lyrics that bridge indie-pop to a soulful blues-rock” [Jeff Milo, DeepCutz 2018]. The Science for Sociopaths online community, hosted on www.patreon.com/scienceforsociopaths and various social media, is the lifeblood of the project. Through this online community Maggie is able to fund, refine, and release regular content ranging from original music and videos to covers and blogs. Shared experience and a dedication to authenticity drive this project, and ensure a meaningful emprise for Maggie and SfS fans alike.

Education

Maggie received her Bachelors in Music Education from Oakland University in Rochester Michigan. Under the tutelage of Deborah Blair - Co-creator of Exceptional pedagogy for children with exceptionalities: International perspectives - and Jackie Wiggins - Teaching for Musical Understanding - Maggie developed a dedicated skillset to match her love of teaching.
